    4. Assumptions & Limitations

    Key Assumptions:

    1. Market Efficiency Assumption: Oil prices reflect all available information at time t
    2. Exogeneity Assumption: Events are external shocks to the market
    3. Structural Break Assumption: Price dynamics change abruptly at detected points
    4. Data Quality Assumption: Historical data is accurate and representative
    5. Model Specification: Normal distribution adequately captures price returns

    Important Limitations:

    1. Causality vs Correlation:
    2. Critical Limitation: Detected change points may correlate with but not necessarily be caused by listed events
    3. Example: A change point might coincide with an event but be driven by unobserved factors

    4. Mitigation: Use event windows and control for other known factors

    5. Multiple Simultaneous Events:

    6. Events often cluster in time (e.g., 2008 financial crisis with OPEC decisions)
    7. Attribution of price changes to specific events is challenging

    8. Approach: Use Bayesian model averaging to assess probabilities

    9. Market Anticipation Effects:

    10. Prices may adjust before official event dates (e.g., sanctions announcements)
    11. Impact: Change points may precede actual event dates

    12. Solution: Include announcement dates where available

    13. Model Simplifications:

    14. Single change point model may miss multiple regime shifts
    15. Normal distribution may not capture fat tails in oil returns

    16. Plan: Extend to multiple change points and student-t distributions

    17. Time Lag Considerations:

    18. Event impacts may unfold over days/weeks, not instantaneously

    19. Approach: Analyze event windows (±30 days) rather than single dates

    20. Confounding Variables:

    21. Macroeconomic factors (GDP, inflation, USD strength)
    22. Technological changes (shale revolution)
    23. Acknowledgment: These are not explicitly modeled in initial analysis

    7. Risk Management

    Technical Risks:

    1. Computational Intensity: MCMC sampling for long time series

    2. Mitigation: Use efficient sampling, reduce tuning steps initially

    3. Model Convergence: Poor mixing or non-convergence

    4. Mitigation: Multiple chains, extensive diagnostics, parameter tuning

    5. Data Quality Issues: Gaps or anomalies in price data

    6. Mitigation: Robust interpolation, outlier detection

    Project Risks:

    1. Scope Creep: Adding too many features

    2. Mitigation: Stick to core Bayesian analysis, defer enhancements

    3. Interpretation Complexity: Explaining Bayesian results to non-technical stakeholders

    4. Mitigation: Clear visualizations, simple language summaries
